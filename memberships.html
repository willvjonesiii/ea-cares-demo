The Math Behind It

Why membership
makes sense.

For a pet receiving monthly Cytopoint injections, the math is immediate. A single vet clinic visit typically includes exam fee plus injection fee — often $250–$350 per visit. At EA Care, that same clinical service comes to you on a schedule.

Your pet stays home

No crate, no car ride, no waiting room. Pets treated in their own environment show lower stress hormones, easier handling, and more accurate baseline vitals.

02

The same LVT, every time

Continuity of care means Estefany knows your pet's normal. She catches changes clinic staff on rotating schedules never would.

Clinical documentation included

Every visit is documented. Notes, vitals, photos, and observations are available for you and forwardable to your veterinarian.

Early detection at every visit

An 8-point wellness check at every visit means small problems get flagged before they become emergencies and expensive interventions.

Common Questions

Everything you need
to know.

No referral is needed to start a Paw Essentials (Tier 1) membership. For clinical services in Tier 2 and Tier 3 — injectable medications, wound care, or fluid therapy — EA Care works under an existing veterinary order. If you don't have a current vet relationship, we can help connect you with a local DVM. All clinical procedures are performed within New York State LVT scope of practice.
EA Care is based in The Bronx and serves all five New York City boroughs — Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Staten Island, and The Bronx. Trip fees are waived for all membership clients. For non-member single visits, a trip fee applies based on distance.
Yes. All memberships are month-to-month with no long-term contract. You can cancel or pause at any time before your next billing cycle. Recovery Care memberships (Tier 3) are offered as structured 3–6 month plans and may have different cancellation terms — these are discussed during enrollment.
Yes. EA Care services are available for both cats and dogs. The services offered, techniques, and handling protocols are adapted for each species. Some services (like certain injectable medications) are used primarily in dogs; Estefany will confirm what's clinically appropriate for your specific pet during intake.
All injectable medications require a current veterinary order. In most cases, your vet prescribes and provides the medication, and Estefany administers it on schedule at your home. She can coordinate directly with your DVM if needed, and documents each administration for the medical record. This is the exact same workflow as a hospital LVT performing the same procedure in-clinic.
If a visit requires services beyond your plan tier, Estefany will discuss it with you before proceeding. Additional services are available at à la carte rates. If she identifies a condition that requires a full DVM examination or emergency care, she will communicate that clearly and help coordinate the appropriate next steps with your veterinarian.
