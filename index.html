Why It Matters

Not just a pet nurse.
A Licensed Veterinary Technician.

A Licensed Veterinary Technician (LVT) completes a rigorous 2–4 year accredited program, passes national board examinations, and holds a state license. This is the same credential held by the person drawing blood, placing IVs, and monitoring anesthesia at your vet's clinic.


The difference between a groomer and an LVT isn't just training — it's legal scope of practice. EA Care brings clinical-grade care directly to your home, under proper veterinary supervision, for a fraction of the stress of a clinic visit.

Clinical services require a valid Veterinarian-Client-Patient Relationship (VCPR) and veterinarian-issued prescription or protocol. EA Care operates under licensed veterinary supervision in full compliance with New York State veterinary practice regulations.
