Clinical Qualifications

What an LVT Can Do
That Others Can't.

A Licensed Veterinary Technician holds state-issued clinical credentials that authorize procedures far beyond what a groomer, pet sitter, or unlicensed tech can legally perform. This is everything EA Care is qualified to do in your home.

The License Explained

LVT vs. everyone
else.

The title Licensed Veterinary Technician (LVT) is a state-issued professional license — not a certificate, not a course completion, not a title anyone can claim. In New York State, it requires completing an AVMA-accredited veterinary technology program and passing the VTNE national board examination.

An LVT is to a DVM what a registered nurse is to a physician. The scope of practice is defined by law and encompasses clinical procedures that are completely outside the legal authority of groomers, pet sitters, dog walkers, or even unlicensed veterinary assistants.

"When an LVT places an IV catheter or administers anesthesia, it isn't a workaround — it's within the exact scope of their license, performed to the same clinical standard as hospital care."

EA Care operates under veterinary supervision for all clinical procedures, as required by New York State law. Estefany coordinates directly with your pet's veterinarian to ensure every service is properly authorized and documented.

The Credential Hierarchy

DVM

Doctor of Veterinary Medicine (DVM)

4-year veterinary degree. Diagnoses, prescribes, performs surgery. Cannot be replicated by any other credential.

LVT

Licensed Veterinary Technician (LVT)

State-licensed clinical professional. Performs procedures, administers medications, operates equipment — all under veterinary supervision.

NY State License Required EA Care
RVT

Registered Veterinary Technician (RVT)

Similar credential in states that use RVT terminology instead of LVT. Same VTNE examination.

VET
ASST

Veterinary Assistant / Groomer

No license required. Cannot legally administer medications, draw blood, place catheters, or perform clinical procedures.

Educational Background

The training behind
every visit.

LVT licensure is built on years of hands-on clinical training across every major species and medical discipline. Here's what that program covers.

Year 1–2

AVMA-Accredited Veterinary Technology Program

Coursework in anatomy, physiology, pharmacology, microbiology, and clinical pathology. Laboratory skills including blood analysis, urinalysis, cytology, and sample preparation.

Year 2–3

Clinical Rotations — Small Animal, Exotic, Emergency

Supervised clinical hours in small animal medicine, surgery, emergency and critical care, anesthesia, dentistry, and radiology. Skills assessment on live patients.

Licensure

VTNE — Veterinary Technician National Examination

A 170-question proctored national board examination covering all clinical competency domains. Passing is required for NY State LVT licensure. Score and license verified through NY State Education Department.

Ongoing

Continuing Education & License Renewal

NY State requires ongoing continuing education for LVT license renewal. Estefany maintains current CE credits in clinical nursing, emergency response, and anesthesia monitoring.
